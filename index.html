In March 2025, Mallinckrodt and Endo agreed to merge in a deal valued at nearly $7 billion, combining two specialty drugmakers that had each emerged from Chapter 11 proceedings driven in large part by opioid-related liabilities. (Financial Times, Reuters)

Coverage from the Financial Times and Reuters reports that Endo shareholders were to receive $80 million in cash and about 49.9% of the combined company, with Mallinckrodt investors owning the remaining 50.1%, and that the new group expected about $3.6 billion in 2025 revenue and a listing on the New York Stock Exchange. (FT, Reuters)

Business Overview

What this site covers

The goal is to present the key facts in a “case-style” structure: who the companies are, what drove their restructurings, the transaction sequence, and how the opioid settlements sit around the post-deal entities.

Mallinckrodt & Endo before the merger

Specialty brands plus large generic opioid exposure

Mallinckrodt plc, based in Ireland, was a major manufacturer of generic opioid pain pills and branded products such as Acthar Gel. It faced lawsuits from states, local governments and others alleging that its generics business helped fuel the U.S. opioid epidemic, and it also faced federal actions over Medicaid rebates on Acthar. (California & North Carolina AGs, DOJ/AG releases, BioPharma Dive)

Endo International plc, headquartered in the U.S., marketed opioids including Opana ER and faced state lawsuits and federal claims over its marketing and sale of opioids. The Department of Justice later described misconduct around the distribution of misbranded Opana ER and obtained large criminal fines and forfeiture from Endo Health Solutions in 2024. (DOJ, Kentucky AG, DOJ press release)

Both companies carried significant debt and used Chapter 11 as the forum to settle opioid and other claims, reset their capital structures and hand substantial equity to lenders and opioid trusts.

Keenova & Par Health after the spin-off

Two distinct businesses with different profiles

Mallinckrodt’s corporate site and industry coverage describe a sequence in which the merged Mallinckrodt–Endo group later spun off its generics and sterile injectables unit as Par Health and rebranded the remaining specialty therapeutics business as Keenova Therapeutics in late 2025. (Mallinckrodt.com, Pharmaphorum, BioSpace)

Pharmaphorum and BioSpace report that Keenova focuses on branded medicines such as Acthar Gel, Xiaflex and Terlivaz, with roughly $1.7 billion in pro forma 2024 sales and around 1,600 employees, while Par Health is an independent generics, API and sterile injectables company with quarterly sales of about $337 million and roughly 4,000 employees. (Pharmaphorum, BioSpace)

The strategic idea, as described in this coverage, is to separate a more research- and brand-driven business from a volume-oriented generics platform, each with its own governance and potential investor base.

Opioid settlements as a backdrop.
State attorneys general summarize Mallinckrodt’s agreement to contribute roughly $1.6–1.725 billion over several years into opioid trusts, and Endo’s agreement to pay hundreds of millions of dollars into the Endo Public Opioid Trust and related settlements, alongside federal criminal fines and forfeiture for misbranding Opana ER. (CA & NC AGs, Endo Public Opioid Trust, DOJ)
Deal-driven “fresh starts.”
Financial Times and Reuters both frame the 2025 merger as a way for two opioid-burdened issuers to grow scale and reposition their portfolios after restructuring, even as the trust structures and settlement obligations remain in place around the new companies. (FT, Reuters)
