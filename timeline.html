Chronology

Key events from 2016–2025

Dates and descriptions are drawn from bankruptcy documents, attorney general summaries, and financial/news reporting linked on the Sources page.

Pre-bankruptcy settlement frameworks 2016–2020
State attorneys general such as those in California and North Carolina describe Mallinckrodt as a major generic opioid manufacturer. Around 2020, they outline a global settlement framework in which Mallinckrodt agrees to pay about $1.6 billion in structured cash payments into opioid abatement trusts for states, local governments and tribes. (CA & NC AGs)
First Mallinckrodt Chapter 11 and MDT II Oct 2020 – Jun 2022
Mallinckrodt files for Chapter 11 in October 2020, citing opioid litigation, Acthar-related disputes and a heavy debt load. (BioPharma Dive) A plan confirmed in early 2022 and effective June 16 2022 creates the Mallinckrodt Opioid Master Disbursement Trust II (MDT II), described on its website as a “hub” trust that receives settlement funds and distributes them to sub-trusts for states, tribes, hospitals and individuals. (MDT II, CA AG)
Endo files for Chapter 11 Aug 2022
Endo International files for Chapter 11 in August 2022 with roughly $8 billion in debt. (Reuters) State AGs and the Endo Public Opioid Trust materials describe settlement structures that will allocate hundreds of millions of dollars to opioid abatement over time. (Endo Public Opioid Trust, CA AG)
Second Mallinckrodt filing and revised deal Aug 2023
After emerging from its first Chapter 11 and making an initial $450 million payment to MDT II, Mallinckrodt misses a subsequent payment and files a second, pre-packaged Chapter 11 case in August 2023. The Washington Post and Reuters report that a new deal is negotiated to reduce the opioid settlement by nearly $1 billion and to cut about $2 billion in other debt, with existing equity cancelled. (Washington Post, Reuters, MDT II)
Mallinckrodt plan confirmation and emergence Oct–Nov 2023
In October 2023, a U.S. bankruptcy court approves Mallinckrodt’s second plan. (Reuters) Davis Polk’s summary notes that Mallinckrodt emerges from Chapter 11 in November 2023 with a capital structure consisting largely of “take-back” debt and equity held by first- and second-lien lenders. (Davis Polk)
Endo’s plan approval and criminal resolution Mar 2024
Reuters reports that in March 2024 a U.S. court approves Endo’s Chapter 11 plan, which transfers more than 95% of the reorganized equity to lenders and dedicates around $600 million to opioid and tax-related settlements. (Reuters) The Department of Justice separately announces over $1.5 billion in criminal fines and forfeiture for misbranded Opana ER, alongside Endo’s contributions to the Endo Public Opioid Trust and other settlements. (DOJ, Endo Public Opioid Trust)
Mallinckrodt–Endo merger announcement Mar 13 2025
Financial Times and Reuters report that Mallinckrodt and Endo agree to merge in a nearly $7 billion transaction. According to these outlets, Endo shareholders are slated to receive $80 million in cash and about 49.9% of the combined company, with Mallinckrodt investors holding the remaining 50.1%, and the new group expects about $3.6 billion in 2025 revenue and a NYSE listing. (FT, Reuters, MarketWatch, Investopedia)
Merger closing Jul 31 2025
Mallinckrodt’s corporate site states that on July 31 2025, Mallinckrodt and Endo “joined together to better serve patients and customers,” marking the closing of the merger. (Mallinckrodt.com) Industry commentary notes that the combined business spans specialty brands as well as a large generics and sterile injectables platform. (Pharmaphorum)
Spin-off of Par Health and Keenova rebrand Nov 2025
Mallinckrodt’s site and media reports from Pharmaphorum, BioSpace and Fierce Pharma describe a subsequent separation in which the generics and sterile injectables business is spun off as Par Health and the remaining specialty therapeutics business adopts the Keenova Therapeutics name. (Mallinckrodt.com, Pharmaphorum, BioSpace, FiercePharma) Pharmaphorum reports that Keenova has pro forma 2024 sales of around $1.7 billion and roughly 1,600 employees, while Par Health records quarterly sales of roughly $337 million with around 4,000 employees. (Pharmaphorum, BioSpace)
