Dates and descriptions below are drawn from bankruptcy-related filings, state attorney general summaries, and financial/news reporting linked in the Sources page.

Condensed narrative timeline

Mallinckrodt, Endo, Keenova & Par Health

Pre-bankruptcy opioid settlements and scrutiny 2016–2020
Mallinckrodt, described by several state attorneys general as one of the largest generic opioid manufacturers in the U.S., negotiates a tentative national settlement framework around 2020, under which it agrees to contribute about $1.6 billion in structured payments to an opioid abatement trust for states, local governments and tribal entities. (California & North Carolina AGs) At the same time, the company faces separate federal and state actions over Medicaid rebates and pricing of its non-opioid Acthar product. (DOJ, state AG releases)
First Mallinckrodt Chapter 11 October 2020 – June 2022
In October 2020, Mallinckrodt files for Chapter 11 protection in the United States. Coverage from BioPharma Dive and others notes that the filing comes “amid U.S. opioid litigation” and Acthar-related pressures. (BioPharma Dive) A plan confirmed in early 2022 and effective June 16 2022 creates the Mallinckrodt Opioid Master Disbursement Trust II (MDT II), a “hub” trust to receive settlement funds and direct them into a network of creditor trusts for governments, tribes, hospitals and individuals. (MDT II, CA AG)
Endo files for Chapter 11 August 2022
Endo International files for Chapter 11 in August 2022, with Reuters and state AGs citing roughly $8 billion in debt and extensive opioid-related exposure tied to products such as Opana ER. (Reuters, CA AG, Kentucky AG) Later, California’s Attorney General and the Endo Public Opioid Trust describe a settlement structure in which hundreds of millions of dollars are dedicated to opioid abatement via a public trust. (Endo Public Opioid Trust, CA AG)
Second Mallinckrodt filing August 2023
After emerging from its first Chapter 11 and making an initial $450 million payment to MDT II, Mallinckrodt misses a subsequent payment and announces a second, pre-packaged Chapter 11 filing in August 2023. Reporting from the Washington Post and Reuters highlights that the new plan will cut nearly $1 billion from the original opioid settlement obligations and sharply reduce other debt, while cancelling existing equity. (Washington Post, Reuters, MDT II)
Mallinckrodt’s second plan confirmed October–November 2023
In October 2023, a U.S. bankruptcy court approves Mallinckrodt’s second plan, cutting about $1 billion from the opioid settlement and trimming nearly $2 billion in other debt. (Reuters) Davis Polk’s deal summary notes that Mallinckrodt emerges from Chapter 11 in November 2023 with a new mix of “take-back” debt and equity held largely by its first- and second-lien lenders. (Davis Polk)
Endo’s Chapter 11 plan and opioid trust funding March 2024
Reuters reports that in March 2024 a U.S. court approves Endo’s Chapter 11 plan, transferring more than 95% of the reorganized equity to lenders and dedicating around $600 million toward opioid and tax-related settlements, including hundreds of millions of dollars for the Endo Public Opioid Trust. (Reuters, CA AG, Endo Public Opioid Trust) DOJ press releases and additional coverage describe a separate criminal case in which Endo Health Solutions is ordered to pay over $1.5 billion in fines and forfeiture related to misbranded Opana ER. (DOJ)
Merger announcement: Mallinckrodt & Endo March 13 2025
Financial Times and Reuters report that Mallinckrodt and Endo agree to merge in a nearly $7 billion transaction. According to these outlets, Endo shareholders are slated to receive $80 million in cash and about 49.9% of the combined company, with Mallinckrodt investors retaining approximately 50.1%, and the new group expects about $3.6 billion in 2025 revenue and a NYSE listing. (FT, Reuters, MarketWatch, Investopedia)
Merger closing July 31 2025
Mallinckrodt’s website states that on July 31 2025, Mallinckrodt and Endo “joined together to better serve patients and customers,” marking the closing of the merger. (Mallinckrodt.com) Industry commentary notes that the combined business includes branded medicines and a large generics and sterile injectables platform. (Pharmaphorum)
Spin-off of Par Health and Keenova rebrand November 2025
Mallinckrodt’s homepage and media reports from Pharmaphorum, BioSpace and Fierce Pharma describe a subsequent separation in which the combined generics and sterile injectables business is spun off as Par Health and the remaining specialty therapeutics business adopts the Keenova Therapeutics name. (Mallinckrodt.com, Pharmaphorum, BioSpace, FiercePharma) Pharmaphorum reports that Keenova has pro forma 2024 sales of around $1.7 billion with roughly 1,600 employees, while Par Health records quarterly sales of roughly $337 million with around 4,000 employees. (Pharmaphorum, BioSpace)
